VERY SOON, YOU WILL FORGET WHAT YOU WERE - Interview with xxignal - by Karen Harker

XXignal are utterly mysterious - a secretive presence at the heart of a culture - the death garage/ katajunglist/cybergoth anti-scene - that is itself little more than a rumour at the moment. But the XXignal style is unmistakable, and immediately infectious. All the trademark hooks - the abyssal bass, clicking tic-swirls, and langorous, icy eroticism - are in place on their only release to date, the Inhumans remixed ³Anti-Climax² on London¹s Katasonix label. It¹s a dark window into a populous netherworld that you enter at your peril...

Meeting XXignal isn¹t easy, and after you¹ve done it, you¹re not exactly sure what¹s happened. In fact, you¹re not sure about much at all...

Getting to XXignal involves a soul-shredding voyage out beyond identity, a trip into the seceded zone of the Net known as the Crypt. The K-goth hang-out is a virtual nightmare - the loa-strewn black mirror of Gibson¹s cyberspace made real, ahead of time. It¹s here - in the strobing, insectile maw of the matrix - that XXignal grant you an audience, if you can call it that. It feels more like possession, trance, takeover. When it¹s happening, nothing makes sense. And when you look back on it later, it only get weirder...

You¹d been given two names - Jean Traffix and Gill Slitz. Obvious pseudonyms ... And, at first, you¹re aware of two presences, but each of them seems impossibly numerous. Everything they say comes from more than one place. Their voices fade into collective chatter: subvocal murmurs, ambient whisperings, electrostatic hum, your own thoughts. ³We¹re an all girl band. That¹s band as in pack. Of course none of us play anything.² You¹re aware - somehow - that this is Gill speaking. Voice trailing off into synthetic chitter. All girl isn¹t very human, you find out very quickly. Their faces - ceaselessly swallowed back into the flickering blue-grey of the delirial corridor - never stay in focus long enough for you to really get a handle on them. You remember only a pallor so improbably pronounced it approaches transluscence. Overwhelming feral glamour. You feel sick with an attraction that isn¹t recognizably sexual. You¹re already getting it. Lesbo-vampiric hunger. Very soon, you will forget what you were.

Asking about influences is obviously foolish. Jean looks at you blankly. Flash of pale blue lights up deep black lagoon eyes. ³Heidi K..² Gill laughs metallically. Jean: ³It¹s about contamination, not influence.² ³Who knows what¹s growing inside us?² Talking about music seems pointless. You¹re learning everything you need to know about tic-cultural contagion simply from being down here, warmth between your legs as you¹re drawn into the deep, low throb of Crypt-carnival, dissolving into electric-agonies indistinguishable from the most intense bliss...

And, before you know it, it¹s gone. You come to in your chair, cold-sweating with junkie hunger. Catching a glimpse of yourself in the mirror, you notice you¹re paler than you¹ve ever been. You want to go back so bad, but for now, all you¹ve got is the sonics. They¹re playing on continuous loop on your minidisc player, the two latest XXignal Crypt-hits, 'Suckers' and 'Utterminus'. Sheer compulsion. You can't resist, and you¹ve given up pretending you even want to...
