The Inhumans
The Inhumans CD 'Hypertension' was recorded between 1996 and 1998 in Manchester and Brighton, England, during bouts of mental instability and urban dread. Even though few have actually heard it, it's already been hailed as the most important debut since The Stooges eponymous platter. Sonically it owes little to anything else around and that - given it's amateurish and primitive UR punk quality - is probably a good thing! Rumour has it The Inhmans have split. Though the next release - pencilled in for 2K - is sensitively titled 'FUCKERZ'. A spokesthing for the band commented: 'We'd just like to say to everyone - especially The Chemical Brothers - you suck!'

1. Fuzz Bomb

Your brains inflamed.'Everyone's out to get you, motherfucker.' Berlin 99 meets Detroit 69 in gabba, gabba hey! mayhem.

2. Roky Erickson

A tribute (of sorts) to the demented genius who walked with a zombie. Spagetti western psych dub country fried acid beats.

3. Subway Terra

Do you use public transport at night when the Starz are out? You could meet demons there. Don't fall down Jacob's ladder.

4. Uber Dub

Matrix style mash up from the Summer of Hate, 1996. Beatz not working.

5. Sinister Street

A field recording from the bleakest streets of the industrial North. They live on fear there...

6. Professor barker

65 million years BC. Some believe Barker to be the Fuzz - I say it's a truth/myth. The unutterable, the worst thought in the world...

Interview with mysterioso UK kult sample-punk combo The Inhumans, Mikstonic Hotel, Dunwich, June 1999.

Conducted by Adam Zero, journalist for US hardcore 'zine, Minimal Self, in conversation with a slimy freak - natch - an Inhuman's representative.

I plug in the tape and press PLAY. It's cloudy outside. Could rain anytime now.

I can barely see their face.

AZ: First, where does the name come from?

I: From Marvel - no, don't print that...too late. Expect the litigation. Fuck! But we've got no money!

AZ (laughing): Cool...Would you say you're Punk?

I: Very much so...I'd like to think you can draw a line way back from The Kingsmen thru tha' Stooges, obviously, to British punk like ATV and US stuff like Chrome to, inevitably, us. Yeah, definitely, but touching other bases in-between, too...

AZ: Nothin' to do with dance, then?

I: No, not with dance as the wider media and public perceives it. No, we've always held that 'disco sucks' and still do. OK we employ samplers and breakbeats but so what!? That's just cos it's easy - easy technology and easy to use. Here's a sample; here's another - now form a band! Saves having to employee real bass players; real assholes...Some genres have been a big influence obviously, Mantronix for sure...Schooly D/Skinny Boys and Renegade soundwave definitely. We rate them as one of the most important bands of the last ten years or so. They invented big beat/punky techno not the fuckin piece of shit, Chemical Brothers...We're not anti dancin' - shit, we can dance to Beefheart, man.

AZ: That's contentious.

I: Too right but we couldn't have it any other way, man. That's what we're about. That's the vision. It may be perverted.

AZ: The album Hypertension is dark. There's a palpable tension there.

I: Sure. It was recorded in some of the bleakest parts of the UK. Real, Sinister Street. The Inhumans were on the verg of a nervous breakdown recording some of those tracks. What you hear is real Dostoevskian fear. If you look long enough into the abyss, the abyss begins to look into you...

AZ: Yeah, it's pretty dark. Even the cover.

I: That's by a friend who's a great painter. Chris Gollon. We own that print used. It's a monochrome from a brass plate, like Blake used to. There was only one printed then the plate destroyed.

He shows me more of Gollon's work. Disturbing. Unholy.

AZ: I think - and it might sound grand - but your music and the Katasonix crew in general are sort of too way out there.

I: Yeah we don't fit in anywhere. No one wants to know which is cool, but we do have a pocket of people who get what we're doing. The music press doesn't wanna know. We're too primitive for The Wire - they like white boy prog tech, know what I mean? There's no fretless bass workouts on our tracks. And as for the NME - shit, they're fucked now. People talk of a rock/dance cross over but it's only on polite terms. We use it as polemic!

AZ: Seeing The Matrix recently, it struck me that Uber Dub is particularly prescient.

I: Would you believe that was the first track we ever did with the sampler? Over 3 years old, man. Why, at the time, everyone's goin' apeshit about superfast beats so we slow the mutha down to less than 35. Like Swans did with hardcore in the late 80s.

The guy's pacing around now. Real fired up.

I: I've not seen that film yet but, yeah, we're five years ahead of our time. Take it from me all the white middle class fuckwits will be there soon.

AZ: What next?

I: Dunno. There may not be a next. Maybe something in 2000. But we might post snippets for ya.

He's serious.

I: It might be web based. No vinyl or CDs. Let people get it for a small fee - free even. We don't care. We don't need the money.

AZ: Perhaps the Internet is the ultimate Punk platform?

I: Yeah, but only the substrata - the Crypt. But, sure, that seemed to be the original intention of the thing before the marketing men got on board. E comm-arse...

AZ: Who needs the E Corps?

I: Precisely. I think the web may become increasingly crucial for us. The middle ground's too staid; too tied up and I don't think anyone's gonna shake it loose.

AZ: That seems a fitting epitaph?

I: Or resurrection!

AZ: Thanks for your time...

I: No, thank you. It's been a disease, maaaann!

So I say goodbye, then, but one lingering thought remains. How DO you shake hands with a tentacle??

I leave the hotel and the street lamps are flickering. Little children congregate in the streets, their faces strangely hidden. No one looks you in the eye in Dunwich - it's as though they've all got a secret to hide. A terrible secret - aeons old...

I catch my plane back.Pan Am.It was on time.
